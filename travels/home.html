Introducing Kerala: Why Choose Kerala as your Next Travel Destination

Fondly referred to as "God's own Country"; Kerala with a vast coastline fringed with coconut palm, extensive stretches of biodiverse Western Ghats and cultural vibrancy is one of the most popular tourist destinations in India as well as Asia. Included in the National Geographic Traveller's list of "50 Places of a Lifetime", Kerala with its equitable climate, long shoreline with over 50 serene beaches, sprawling tea plantations and spice fields, extensive network of canals and waterchannels popularly known as Kerala backwaters, diverse wildlife and the ancient art of healing Ayurveda has intoxicated travelers since the time of Greeks who sailed to Malabar Coast in search of riches and pleasure.

Places We Cover

Wayanad:

W

ayanad – Perfect place to unwind in the lap of nature. Wayanad is a haven for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those looking to connect with the serene beauty of the Western Ghats. Whether you're interested in trekking through scenic trails, exploring ancient caves, visiting breathtaking waterfalls, or simply enjoying the cool, refreshing climate, Wayanad has something for everyone. The region's rich cultural heritage and spice plantations add to the allure, making it a perfect destination for both adventure and relaxation.

Alleppey (Alappuzha):

A

"lappuzha, where the land meets the water, and every sunset feels like a poem written on the horizon." Alappuzha is famous for its backwaters and houseboat cruises. The city is renowned for its scenic backwaters, stunning beaches, vibrant culture, and natural beauty. It is a perfect getaway for those who seek a peaceful and relaxing escape from their daily routine. Whether you want to explore the backwaters, indulge in the local culture, or simply relax on the beaches, Alleppey has something for everyone.

Kumarakom:
K

"umarakom, where serene backwaters meet lush greenery, and every houseboat ride feels like a journey through paradise." Kumarakom is a serene village located on the banks of Vembanad Lake, renowned for its scenic backwaters and tranquil environment. It is a paradise for bird watchers, with the "Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary" attracting a variety of migratory birds. Visitors can enjoy leisurely houseboat cruises through the picturesque backwaters, surrounded by lush greenery and traditional Kerala village life. The village is also known for its luxury resorts and Ayurvedic treatments, offering a relaxing retreat amidst nature.

Kochi (Cochin):
K

"ochi, where history meets the sea, and every street tells a story of culture and heritage." Kochi, also known as Cochin, is a vibrant city on Kerala's southwest coast, renowned for its rich history and diverse cultural influences. "Fort Kochi" showcases colonial architecture and the historic Chinese fishing nets, creating a unique blend of cultures. The "Mattancherry Palace", with its stunning murals and artifacts, reflects the city's royal heritage. "Jew Town" offers a glimpse into Kochi's Jewish history and bustling markets. The "Kerala Folklore Museum" provides insight into the region's arts and traditions. With its lively harbor, eclectic dining, and dynamic arts scene, Kochi is a bustling gateway to Kerala’s cultural tapestry.

Palakad:
P

alakkad, often called the "Gateway to Kerala," is a picturesque district known for its lush landscapes and historical sites. The "Palakkad Fort" offers a glimpse into the region's past with its well-preserved walls and moat. The "Parambikulam Tiger Reserve" provides a haven for wildlife, including tigers and elephants. The "Nelliyampathi Hills" are renowned for their tea plantations and breathtaking views. The "Silent Valley National Park" is a pristine rainforest with rich biodiversity, perfect for nature enthusiasts.

Munnar:

M

"unnar, where mist kisses the hills and tea gardens weave green dreams in the dawn's quiet embrace." Munnar is a breathtaking hill station nestled in the Western Ghats, renowned for its sprawling tea plantations, misty mountains, and cool climate. Visitors can explore stunning viewpoints, lush green valleys, and beautiful waterfalls, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and honeymooners. The region's rich biodiversity, including the rare Neelakurinji flowers and unique wildlife, adds to its enchanting charm.
